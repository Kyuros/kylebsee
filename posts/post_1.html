Creating "Good" Scientific Figures

Date: February 27, 2023

By: Kyle See

Why do we care? Scientific figures are an important piece of any publication. Figures are often among the first things that a reader will look at when reading a paper. They are crafted to convey complex information at a glance. Almost any publication with data analysis has some sort of figure. Figures take advantage of our ability to easily recognize patterns (trends, gaps, outliers). Conversely, poor graphics can lead to confusing or misleading interpretations. A disorganized figure will not convey any useful information. Therefore, it is crucial to understand the basics in creating coherent and engaging figures!

Data visualization is not only important in academic pursuits, but also translatable to other careers. For example, teachers can view student results to better understand and develop teaching plans. Computer scientists can visualize their data and novel concepts to convince peers and shareholders to invest in their research. Businesses can quickly identify growing trends to capitalize on the market.

What do figures provide? While figures are essential to any data analysis, it is important to understand what they exactly provide. Figures in scientific communication allow us to:

  • Gain insight into exploratory data analysis
  • Document and visualize results from experiments
  • Checking data quality and structure before use
  • Making comparisons between data
  • Form inferences about the data
  • Identifying outliers
  • Visualize complex methodologies
